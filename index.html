Flagstaff Hill

17 July 2021

This is one of my favorite memories. Alex and I sat at the top of this hill under a row of broad oak trees, protected from the light, on-and-off drizzle, admiring the rolling green hill and the city soaring above the trees. The cool, gentle breeze and the tumbling, overcast clouds made the atmosphere feel magical. The desolate campus made it feel like a bubble suspended in time and place. I don't remember entirely what we talked about, but it was an experience I deeply cherish.

Grandview Overlook or Paul F. Jones Overlook

17 July 2021

After dinner back together, we ended our trip to Pittsburgh by visiting one of the overlooks (I think Grandview Overlook, but definitely it or Paul F. Jones Overlook) on the steep south bank of the Monongahela River for an incredible view of the Pittsburgh skyline. The memory this picture reminds me of is being jeered at by a stranger as we got out of the car because we filled every seat in Blaise's car.

Dolly Sods

A day trip to the Dolly Sods Wilderness while I was a NSF REU student at WVU

Somewhere between Morgantown and Dolly Sods

3 July 2021

We took the highway east from Morgantown and briefly passed through the Maryland panhandle before turning south toward Dolly Sods. That day, we drove through more than one volunteer fire department donation drive in the small towns we went through. I'm not sure if they were coincidental, or if they were connected to the holiday the next day.
